Sushi the cuisine

About sushi

Sushi the delicate cuisine modernized sometime around the mid-1800's. Some say that sushi originated in Southeast Asia and eventually landed on plates in Japan! While the sushi we eat now days is made with sweet sticky rice, back then the rice was fermented and discarded before eating to keep the fish preserved!

Sushi styles

Sashimi

For the purests pleasure sashimi is only raw fish. Each piece of sashimi is sculpted perfectly to delicately displayed its natural beauty. A plate of this treat can have many varieties of seafood.

Nigiri

Nigiri one of the oldest forms of sushi. This is fish over rice. Very simple and tasty. Typically a perfect amount of wasabi is put between the rice and fish to compliment the two flavors.

Maki

Maki is a traditional style roll, maki meaning roll in Japanese. This is a form of sushi that adds the incredient of seaweed. The rice is spread on the seaweed and the ingredients are placed inside rolled with the seaweed towards the outside. This roll is then cut into bite size pieces.

Temaki

Te(hand)-maki(roll) is a cone shaped similar to an ice cream cone. Inside is packed full of ingredients. The seaweed is placed on the outside while rice and ingredients are rolled inside. This is ment to be eaten with your hands and no need for utensilse.

Roll

This "roll" style sushi is what most people consider to be "sushi". Probably the most famous roll that most people know of is the California roll. This style sushi is made with the rice on the outside of the roll and ingredients placed inside and on top of the roll. With this style there is almost endless possibilities.

Tools of the trade

There are many tools that are unique to sushi. Each tool has a key role in the art of preparing sushi. These are things you wont find in a everyday kitchen.

Type of tools

Sashimi knives

Specific to the shape of this knife it is flat on one side and angled on the other to slide the fish off the knife with a simple twist. When used to cut several pieces of sashimi the sashimi will stack up perfectly next to eachother so that the sushi chef can pick it up place it on the plate in one motion.

Makisu

This is a bamboo mat that is used to shape the sushi rolls. The rolls are simply placed underneath and given a little pressure to give it a round or square shape. The makisu is also used to make makis. Probably one of the most used tools next to the knife.

Neta case

The neta case is the display case that holds all the different types of fish. Its almost completely glass with sliding doors and several referigeration bars. This is what keeps the sushi at the correct temperature.

Hangiri tub

This tub is made of pine and is used as a container to mix the rice vinegar with large batchs of rice. The hangiri designed to hold the moisture as the steamed rice cools off. This is one of the most important tools because rice is the most important part of sushi.
